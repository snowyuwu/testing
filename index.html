Case Presentation

A 60-year-old woman who lives in Massachusetts presented to the emergency department in March 2020 with a 5-day history of fevers and chills. She reported no rhinorrhea, sore throat, or loss of smell (anosmia) and no focal neurologic symptoms. She had no rashes or urinary symptoms.

The patient recalled no sick contacts, but she had traveled to the United Kingdom 1 month before presentation and to Westchester County, New York, 1 week before presentation. Westchester County had since been recognized as having an outbreak of Covid-19.

She was admitted to the hospital during her visit to the emergency department.

Patient History
Medical History :
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Childhood asthma
Medications :

Levothyroxine, 50 µg, every morning

Allergies :

No known drug allergies

Social History :
  • Lives alone in western Massachusetts
  • Works as a professor at a university
  • Runs regularly and engages in other athletic activities
  • Reports no use of tobacco, alcohol, or intravenous drugs
Family History :
  • Father with pulmonary fibrosis
  • Brother with lung disease
Physical Examination
Vital signs and general appearance :
  • Temperature, 39.2° C
  • Heart rate, 87 beats per minute
  • Blood pressure, 108/70 mm Hg
  • Respiratory rate, 16 breaths per minute
  • Oxygen saturation, 92% while breathing ambient air
  • Not in acute distress but appears fatigued
Lungs :
  • Bibasilar crackles
  • No wheezing or rhonchi
  • No tenderness to percussion
  • No use of accessory muscles for breathing or respiration
